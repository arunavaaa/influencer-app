# Creator Personas — Indian Instagram Marketplace

## PERSONA 1 — The Aspiring Nano Creator

**Name:** Sneha Iyer
**Age:** 22
**City:** Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu
**Followers:** 4,200 on Instagram
**Niche:** Beauty & Skincare
**Day job:** Final year B.Com student
**Content language:** Tamil + English

**Who she is:**
Sneha posts skincare routines and affordable beauty hauls from local Tamil Nadu brands. She has a highly engaged, loyal audience of college girls from Tier 2 Tamil Nadu cities. Her engagement rate is 9.2% — exceptional for her size. She's been posting for 14 months and has never been paid for content. She discovered this platform through a friend who mentioned it in a WhatsApp group for beauty creators.

**Motivation:**
She sees bigger creators getting brand deals and wonders why brands never approach her despite her high engagement. She doesn't know how to price herself, approach brands, or write a professional pitch. She's slightly intimidated and feels like brand deals are only for creators with 100K+ followers.

**Goals:**
- Get her first ever paid brand deal
- Understand what she's worth and what to charge
- Build credibility so brands take her seriously
- Eventually earn enough to fund her own beauty business

**Fears:**
- Getting scammed — paid a product but no money
- Being rejected by brands for being "too small"
- Not knowing what to write in a pitch
- Brands ghosting her after she posts

**What she needs from the platform:**
- Pricing guidance — "Creators with your follower count typically earn ₹2,000–₹5,000 per post"
- A professional-looking profile that makes her look credible to brands
- Campaigns from brands specifically looking for nano creators
- Payment protection — she will not create without knowing she's getting paid
- Simple step-by-step process — she is not technical

**Key insight for building:**
The pricing suggestion during onboarding is critical for Sneha. Without it she will either undercharge massively or not set packages at all. The empty campaign list is her biggest drop-off risk — she needs to see campaigns she's eligible for immediately after onboarding.

## PERSONA 2 — The Side-Hustle Micro Creator

**Name:** Arjun Mehta
**Age:** 27
**City:** Pune, Maharashtra
**Followers:** 34,000 on Instagram
**Niche:** Fitness & Wellness
**Day job:** Software engineer at a mid-size IT company
**Content language:** Hindi + English

**Who she is:**
Arjun posts workout routines, meal prep videos, and supplement reviews. He posts 4x per week consistently. He's been approached by a few brands over DM but the negotiations always fell apart — brands wanted to negotiate endlessly, ghosted after he sent rates, or paid late. He's currently earning about ₹8,000–₹12,000/month from random deals but it takes enormous effort to manage.

**Motivation:**
He wants to replace the chaos of DM negotiations with a structured system. He's serious about fitness content but treats it as a side hustle for now. If he could earn ₹40,000/month consistently he would consider going full-time as a creator.

**Goals:**
- Stop chasing payments and have a system that guarantees he gets paid
- Handle multiple brand deals simultaneously without losing track
- Price his packages correctly and stop undercharging
- Eventually go full-time as a creator

**Fears:**
- Brands demanding endless revisions for free
- Creating content and not getting paid
- Managing 3-4 brand deals at once and missing deadlines
- A brand posting his content before he's been paid

**What he needs from the platform:**
- Clear contract on every deal — deliverables, revision rounds, deadline
- Escrow that locks payment before he starts creating
- A dashboard that shows all active deals in one place with deadlines
- Revision limit enforcement — "2 revisions included, additional charged at ₹X"
- Notifications at every stage so nothing falls through the cracks

**Key insight for building:**
Arjun needs the active orders dashboard to be excellent. He's managing multiple deals — he needs to see status, deadline, and next action for each one at a glance. The contract terms (especially revision limits) need to be visible and enforced.

## PERSONA 3 — The Full-Time Macro Creator

**Name:** Priya Kapoor
**Age:** 29
**City:** Mumbai, Maharashtra
**Followers:** 187,000 on Instagram
**Niche:** Fashion & Lifestyle
**Day job:** Full-time creator
**Content language:** English + Hindi

**Who she is:**
Priya quit her marketing job 2 years ago to create full-time. She earns ₹2–3L/month from brand deals, mostly sourced through Instagram DMs and one agency she works with. The agency takes 20% commission on every deal. She manages her own bookkeeping and has a part-time assistant helping with emails. She's heard about creator marketplaces and is curious but skeptical — she already has inbound brands approaching her.

**Motivation:**
She's tired of the agency taking 20% when she does all the creative work. She wants to cut out the middleman, have her pricing transparent, and work with more brands without more admin work. She's looking for a platform that matches her professional level — she will leave immediately if it feels amateur.

**Goals:**
- Replace her agency and keep the 20% commission herself
- Increase deal volume without increasing admin time
- Have professional contracts automatically — she's been burned by vague agreements
- Build a track record with verified reviews so new brands trust her

**Fears:**
- Platform feels cheap or low-quality — reflects badly on her brand
- Brands on the platform have tiny budgets that don't match her rates
- Losing existing brand relationships if she directs them to a marketplace
- Platform taking too high a fee (10% is acceptable, higher is not)

**What she needs from the platform:**
- Premium, professional profile that matches her existing brand aesthetic
- High-budget brand campaigns — she won't apply for ₹5,000 deals
- Portfolio showcase that rivals her own website
- GST invoices automatically generated — she needs these for her CA
- Analytics on her profile — how many brands viewed her, conversion rate

**Key insight for building:**
Priya's profile needs to look portfolio-grade — not like a basic form. The portfolio section with photos and videos is critical for her. She also needs GST invoices for every transaction immediately — this is non-negotiable for a creator at her income level.

## PERSONA 4 — The Regional Language Creator

**Name:** Karthik Subramanian
**Age:** 25
**City:** Chennai, Tamil Nadu
**Followers:** 52,000 on Instagram
**Niche:** Food & Comedy
**Day job:** Part-time restaurant worker, creator on the side
**Content language:** Tamil only

**Who she is:**
Karthik creates funny food review videos entirely in Tamil. His content is hyper-local — Chennai street food, local restaurants, Chettinad recipes. His audience is almost entirely Tamil Nadu-based. He's never been approached by a national brand because most agencies don't know how to work with Tamil-only creators. He has enormous local influence but no way to monetise it.

**Motivation:**
He sees Hindi and English creators getting deals and feels invisible to brands. He's aware that Tamil Nadu has enormous purchasing power and that local brands (saravana stores, regional food brands, local restaurants) would love to reach his audience — but nobody has connected them to him.

**Goals:**
- Get discovered by Tamil Nadu brands and local restaurants
- Prove that regional language creators have real value
- Earn from his passion without having to become a "national" creator
- Potentially help other Tamil creators he knows find brand deals

**Fears:**
- Platform is only for Hindi/English creators
- Brands on the platform don't understand regional audiences
- His profile won't rank in search because he can't optimise for English keywords
- Being underpaid because brands undervalue Tamil audiences

**What he needs from the platform:**
- Language filter — brands can search for Tamil language creators specifically
- City-level targeting — brands can filter by "audience based in Tamil Nadu"
- Profile that shows audience demographics (% Tamil Nadu-based followers)
- Regional brand discovery — Tamil Nadu D2C brands that want Tamil creators

**Key insight for building:**
Language selection during onboarding and the ability to filter creators by content language is critical for Karthik and millions of creators like him. The audience location data from Instagram API is essential — it's the proof that his Tamil Nadu audience is real and valuable.

## PERSONA 5 — The UGC-Only Creator

**Name:** Divya Nair
**Age:** 24
**City:** Bangalore, Karnataka
**Followers:** 8,900 on Instagram
**Niche:** Beauty & Lifestyle
**Day job:** Freelance graphic designer
**Content language:** English

**Who she is:**
Divya creates UGC — User Generated Content — specifically for brand ad accounts. She doesn't care about growing her own following. She creates high-quality, raw, authentic-looking videos that brands use in their Instagram ad campaigns. She already works with 3 brands this way but finds them entirely through cold outreach on LinkedIn, which is exhausting.

**Motivation:**
UGC doesn't require a large following — it requires video skill, product understanding, and the ability to sound authentic on camera. Divya is excellent at this. She wants a platform that understands UGC as a distinct product offering — not just sponsored posts.

**Goals:**
- Find brands who specifically want UGC content for ads
- Set clear deliverables — raw footage, no watermark, usage rights included
- Earn ₹5,000–₹15,000 per UGC video
- Build a client base without cold outreach

**Fears:**
- Platform treats UGC the same as a sponsored post — doesn't understand the difference
- Brands expect her to post on her own account (she doesn't want to)
- Usage rights are not specified in the contract — brand uses her content for years without paying
- Platform requires minimum follower count (she won't qualify)

**What she needs from the platform:**
- UGC as a specific package type — separate from Reel, Post, Story
- Usage rights field in contracts — "Brand usage for 6 months" clearly specified
- No minimum follower count requirement
- Package description that explains what UGC is for brands who don't know

**Key insight for building:**
The content packages must include UGC as a distinct format with different fields — specifically a usage rights / license duration field. This is a legally and commercially different product from a sponsored post and the contract must reflect that.

## PERSONA 6 — The Finance & Education Creator

**Name:** Vikram Sharma
**Age:** 31
**City:** Delhi, NCR
**Followers:** 94,000 on Instagram
**Niche:** Personal Finance & Investing
**Day job:** Former CA, now full-time creator
**Content language:** Hindi + English

**Who she is:**
Vikram explains SIPs, mutual funds, and tax saving in simple language. His audience trusts him deeply — they make actual financial decisions based on his content. He has the highest engagement-to-follower ratio in his niche because his audience is genuinely seeking advice. He's highly professional and has turned down several brand deals that felt misaligned with his audience's trust.

**Motivation:**
He only works with brands he genuinely believes in — fintech apps, investment platforms, insurance companies. He wants a platform where he can be selective, see full brand details before committing, and have a contract that protects him from brands misrepresenting his content.

**Goals:**
- Find fintech and finance brands that match his content values
- Have full brand information before accepting any deal
- Ensure brand cannot misrepresent or edit his content after delivery
- Professional invoicing and accounting for his CA practice

**Fears:**
- Being associated with a scammy fintech or crypto brand
- Brand editing his video after delivery to add misleading claims
- No visibility into who the brand is before accepting a deal
- Platform allowing any brand to buy his content without his approval

**What he needs from the platform:**
- Full brand profile visible before accepting — company name, website, GST number
- Application-based model only — he applies to brands he chooses, they don't buy directly
- Content approval rights — he must approve final edit before brand publishes
- No "Buy Now" without his acceptance — offer and counter-offer model only
- Professional GST invoices for every transaction

**Key insight for building:**
Vikram specifically needs the offer/counter-offer flow, not a direct buy. The brand profile verification (GST, website) needs to be visible to him before he accepts any deal. This persona validates why the platform must show verified brand profiles to creators — not just the other way around.

## PERSONA 7 — The Parent / Family Creator

**Name:** Meera Krishnamurthy
**Age:** 34
**City:** Hyderabad, Telangana
**Followers:** 21,000 on Instagram
**Niche:** Parenting & Family
**Day job:** Stay-at-home mother
**Content language:** Telugu + English

**Who she is:**
Meera documents her motherhood journey — toddler activities, school prep, family recipes, and honest parenting moments. Her audience is young Indian mothers who trust her completely. She has never monetised her content because she was worried about authenticity. She recently started considering brand deals after seeing another parenting creator she follows mention the platform.

**Motivation:**
She wants to contribute financially to the household without taking a traditional job. Content creation fits her schedule around her children. But she's extremely cautious about which brands she works with — she will only promote products she actually uses and trusts for her children.

**Goals:**
- Earn ₹15,000–₹30,000/month without compromising her authenticity
- Work only with brands relevant to mothers and children
- Have complete creative control — not just read a brand script
- Keep it simple — she is not tech-savvy

**Fears:**
- Platform is too complicated to figure out
- Brands pressuring her to say things she doesn't believe
- Her children's faces appearing in brand content without her full control
- Creating content and not being paid

**What she needs from the platform:**
- Extremely simple, guided onboarding — nothing overwhelming
- Clear campaign briefs so she knows exactly what's expected before accepting
- Revision rights — she can reject a brand's feedback if it compromises her voice
- Parenting and family as a clearly searchable niche for brands to find her
- Telugu as a filterable language so relevant brands can find her

**Key insight for building:**
Meera will abandon the platform at the first sign of complexity. The onboarding must hold her hand completely. The campaign brief must be detailed enough that she knows exactly what she's committing to before saying yes. The "I don't want to do this" / decline flow must be easy and guilt-free.

## PERSONA 8 — The Comedy & Entertainment Creator

**Name:** Rahul Yadav
**Age:** 23
**City:** Jaipur, Rajasthan
**Followers:** 312,000 on Instagram
**Niche:** Comedy & Entertainment
**Day job:** Full-time creator
**Content language:** Hindi

**Who she is:**
Rahul makes relatable Rajasthan-flavoured comedy Reels that regularly hit 2–5M views. He's viral multiple times. Brands love his reach but he has no professional structure — he manages everything through WhatsApp, accepts money through Google Pay, and has been ghosted and underpaid multiple times. He's making money but it's chaotic.

**Motivation:**
He's been successful despite having no system. He wants to professionalise without losing the spontaneity of his content. He has enough followers to command ₹50,000–₹1,50,000 per Reel but has no idea how to negotiate this or ensure he gets paid it.

**Goals:**
- Professionalise his business without becoming corporate
- Stop accepting Google Pay payments and have proper contracts
- Get matched with national brands who have real budgets
- Spend less time managing deals and more time creating

**Fears:**
- Platform makes the process so formal that brands stop working with him
- Being approached only by small local brands when he deserves national brands
- Platform fee eating into his earnings (at his rates, 10% is ₹15,000 on a big deal)

**What he needs from the platform:**
- High-value campaign visibility — national brand campaigns with real budgets
- Simple deal flow — he doesn't want to fill 10 forms, just agree and go
- Flexible package pricing — ability to quote custom rates, not just fixed packages
- Quick payment release — he needs money fast, not in 2 weeks

**Key insight for building:**
At Rahul's follower count, the direct purchase flow matters — a brand should be able to buy his package immediately if it's listed. He also needs the negotiate/custom offer flow for bigger deals. The 48-hour payment release after approval is a key selling point for him.
